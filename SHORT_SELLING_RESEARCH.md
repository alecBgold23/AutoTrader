### Primary Setup: FIRST RED DAY SHORT

**What it is:** After a multi-day runner (stock up 100-300%+ over several consecutive green days), you short on the FIRST day the stock closes red. The thesis is that the momentum has exhausted, late buyers are trapped, and the unwind begins.

**Specific Entry Criteria:**
1. Stock must have had multiple consecutive green days with significant % gains
2. Stock must be showing the first meaningful red candle / failure to make new highs
3. Previous day's close is the KEY level -- it acts as the psychological inflection point

**Entry Mechanics:**
- **Starter position (30% of max size):** Enter when stock breaks below the low of day early in the session
- **Add size (to 60-80%):** When stock breaks below the previous day's close, confirming the First Red Day
- **Full size (100%):** Only below VWAP on A+ setups

**Scaling Rules:**
- Only use 30% of max position size ABOVE VWAP
- If max size = 10,000 shares, only 3,000 shares above VWAP
- Remaining 7,000 shares added when/if it breaks under VWAP
- On A+ setups, may go full size immediately below VWAP

**Stop Loss:**
- Setup is INVALIDATED if price reclaims the previous day's close and HOLDS above it
- The red-to-green reclaim level is the hard stop
- If it reclaims and holds = get out, no exceptions

**Targets:**
- Cover into the panic / flush (when selling accelerates)
- Scale out in pieces as the move develops
- No fixed target -- read the tape for exhaustion of selling

**What Makes It Work Structurally:**
- Late buyers from the final green day(s) are trapped long and become forced sellers
- The break below prior close triggers stop losses cascading
- Short sellers who were squeezed on the way up now have confirmation to re-enter
- Creates a feedback loop: trapped longs selling + new shorts = accelerating downside

### Secondary Setup: GAP DOWN SHORT

**For stocks that gap down after the multi-day run:**
- Watch for a bounce back toward the previous close
- If that bounce FAILS and price rejects that level, that's the entry
- Same stop logic: invalidated if it reclaims and holds above prior close

### Primary Setup: PARABOLIC SHORT (Backside)

**What it is:** Wait for a stock to go parabolic (exponential price acceleration), then short the BACKSIDE of the move after confirmation of reversal. NOT trying to pick the top.

**Specific Entry Criteria:**
1. Stock must be in a true parabolic move (exponential acceleration, not just a strong uptrend)
2. DO NOT short the front side -- wait for the move to exhaust
3. Wait for CONFIRMATION of reversal (specific candle patterns, volume shift)
4. Enter on the backside where risk is DEFINED

**VWAP as Risk Guide:**
- If stock is under VWAP and you are short, use VWAP as your risk level
- If it reclaims VWAP, your thesis is weakening
- VWAP gives structure to what would otherwise be an undefined-risk trade

**Scaling Strategy:**
1. Start with a small "feeler" position (test the waters)
2. Add ONLY when thesis confirms (price action agrees with you)
3. Lock in profit along the way to reduce emotional pressure
4. Never go full size immediately

**Intraday Time Structure:**
- 9:30 AM: React to market open, scalp trades based on pre-market plan
- 10:00-11:00 AM: Watch for breakout and FADE setups (this is prime short territory)
- 11:00 AM-1:00 PM: Step back, only take reactive trades on news or intraday parabolic moves
- Afternoon: Reduced activity unless clear setup presents

**Stop Loss:**
- Defined by the confirmation candle or VWAP level
- If you wait for the right entry, risk becomes structured and defined
- Never short into strength without confirmation

**What Makes It Work Structurally:**
- Parabolic moves are unsustainable by definition -- they MUST reverse
- The question is only WHEN, not IF
- By waiting for the backside, you avoid the squeeze risk that kills most short sellers
- VWAP provides institutional reference point for risk management
- Level 2 reading gives real-time insight into order flow and supply/demand

## COMMON PATTERNS ACROSS ALL VERIFIED TRADERS

### Universal Short Setups (Used by Multiple Traders Above):

**1. First Red Day / Green-to-Red**
- Used by: Temiz, Grittani, Kyle Williams
- Core idea: Multi-day runner exhausts, first red day = momentum shift
- Entry: Break below previous close
- Stop: Reclaim of previous close

**2. Parabolic Short (Backside)**
- Used by: Michaud, Madaz, SMB Capital
- Core idea: Exponential moves must revert; short AFTER confirmation, not before
- Entry: After reversal confirmation on the backside
- Stop: VWAP reclaim or new high

**3. VWAP Rejection Short**
- Used by: SMB Capital, Michaud, Temiz (as size trigger)
- Core idea: VWAP = institutional fair value; rejection = sellers in control
- Entry: Failed attempt to reclaim VWAP
- Stop: Above VWAP

**4. Morning Pop / Gap Up Fade**
- Used by: Madaz, Grittani
- Core idea: Opening bell FOMO creates unsustainable spike; fade the pop
- Entry: When the morning pop stalls and fails
- Stop: Above the pop high

### Universal Risk Management Rules:

1. **NEVER average down on a losing short** (Grittani's Golden Rule, echoed by all)
2. **Scale in, don't go full size at entry** (Temiz 30/70 rule, Williams 20-30/60-80 rule, Michaud feeler positions)
3. **Define risk BEFORE entry** (every trader has a specific invalidation level)
4. **Maximum attempt limits** (Williams: 3-5 max attempts per setup)
5. **VWAP as institutional reference** (used by nearly every trader above)
6. **Liquidity is non-negotiable** (Grittani, Temiz -- only trade stocks you can get out of)
7. **Time of day matters** (10-11 AM is prime short territory; avoid 11 AM-1 PM dead zone)

### What Makes Short Selling Work Structurally (The Real Edge):

1. **Gravity is on your side.** Stocks that go up 300% in a week will give back 50-80% of that move. The statistical reversion is real.
2. **Trapped longs create forced selling.** Late buyers who bought the top become forced sellers as the move reverses, creating accelerating downside.
3. **Promotions end.** Many small-cap runners are pump-and-dumps. When the promotion stops, there are literally no buyers. Price discovery to the downside is violent.
4. **Short squeezes create the setup.** The squeeze that runs the stock up also creates the overextension that makes the short profitable on the way down.
5. **Retail FOMO is predictable.** The last wave of buyers (the FOMO crowd) always arrives at the worst time. They become the exit liquidity for the short.

## CRITICAL WARNINGS FOR IMPLEMENTATION

1. **Borrow availability.** You can't short a stock if shares aren't available to borrow. This is the #1 practical constraint. Brokers: Centerpoint Securities, Cobra Trading, and Interactive Brokers have the best locate inventory for small caps.

2. **Short sale locate fees.** Hard-to-borrow stocks charge locate fees that can be $0.01-$1.00+ per share. This must be factored into your R:R calculation.

3. **SSR (Short Sale Restriction).** When a stock drops 10%+ from the prior close, SSR kicks in and you can only short on upticks. This changes execution significantly.

4. **Unlimited risk.** Unlike longs where max loss is 100%, shorts have theoretically infinite loss. This is why EVERY trader above uses defined stops and scaling.

5. **Pre-borrow vs. locate.** Pre-borrows (securing shares overnight) are more reliable than intraday locates. Plan ahead.

6. **The course seller problem.** Every trader listed above sells education. The question is whether their trading income exceeds their education income. Temiz and Grittani have the strongest cases for trading being primary income based on verified P&L numbers. Michaud's chatroom is a significant business. SMB Capital is definitively a real trading firm.
